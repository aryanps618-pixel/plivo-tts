Fitness function: mean Resemblyzer cosine similarity across 5-8 sentences spanning different phoneme lengths, plus 0.15x cross-sentence embedding consistency (same identity across sentences, not a fluke on one), minus 0.1x a naturalness penalty combining spectral flatness, silence ratio, and duration sanity checks (no ASR/extra model used, per the rules).
Search perturbs only the tensor rows actually exercised by the fitness sentences (found via phoneme count, since Kokoro indexes exactly one row per synthesis call), with separate step sizes for the acoustic half of the style vector (dims 0-128, drives timbre/similarity) and the prosodic half (dims 128-256, drives duration/pitch, kept conservative to protect intelligibility).
Best result: raw speaker similarity 0.6750 vs the best-single-stock-voice baseline of 0.6252, using an adaptive-step random walk (1/5-success rule) over two runs totaling 250 iterations.
Similarity plateaued because the voice tensor has 510 rows but only one is used per synthesis call (indexed by phoneme count), so the search can only ever optimize the narrow band of rows touched by the fitness sentences; step size also decayed under the adaptive rule, converging into a local optimum rather than exhaustively exploring the space.
